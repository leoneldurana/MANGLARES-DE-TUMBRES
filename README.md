## Section 2: Problem Description & Comparative Analysis (Evidence-Based)

Mangrove forests are among the most carbon-dense ecosystems on Earth, yet they remain among the most threatened. Coastal blue carbon ecosystems cover an estimated 50 million hectares worldwide, and roughly half of the planet's historical mangrove cover has already been lost. The Santuario Nacional Los Manglares de Tumbes covers 2,972 hectares and conserves the largest extension of mangrove forest in Peru; scientific estimates place average carbon storage at 366.61 megagrams of carbon per hectare across the Tumbes mangrove system, rising to 458.72 megagrams per hectare within the protected sanctuary, for a total of approximately 1,786,486 megagrams of carbon stored — equivalent to roughly 6.55 million tonnes of CO2. Despite this, the ecosystem continues to be degraded by illegal deforestation for shrimp-farming ponds (langostineras), which releases previously sequestered carbon back into the atmosphere.

The bottleneck is not solely ecological; it is informational and financial. Coastal cooperatives that depend on the mangrove — through mangrove crab, shrimp, and black-shell (concha negra) harvesting — are structurally excluded from the value chain of the carbon credits generated by conservation efforts. This is not a uniquely Peruvian problem: voluntary carbon markets worldwide face the same structural gap between conservation claims and verifiable, community-anchored value distribution, directly implicating **SDG 13 (Climate Action)** and **SDG 14 (Life Below Water)**.

## Section 3: Solution Summary (Executive Abstract)

Blue-Carbon TTF is a multijurisdictional tokenization protocol, deployed on the LACNet EVM-compatible testnet, that converts drone- and satellite-verified mangrove restoration data into Transferable Tokenized Framework (TTF) credits denominated in tCO2eq. Each minting event is distributed automatically by smart contract across coastal communities, technology upkeep, MRV verification, and mangrove nurseries, replacing opaque centralized carbon registries with an immutable, auditable on-chain ledger. The platform is designed for horizontal scalability, allowing additional mangrove jurisdictions to join the same protocol without renegotiating centralized registry agreements. The expected socio-environmental value is a transparent, community-anchored blue carbon economy that can scale from the Tumbes corridor to other mangrove ecosystems across Latin America.

## Section 4: Technologies Used & Technical Architecture

### Tech Stack

| Layer | Technology | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Smart Contracts | Solidity | Implements the MangroveCarbonToken.sol logic: TTF minting, jurisdictional parameters, and circular-economy fund distribution. |
| Development Environment | Remix EVM (IDE) | Web-based environment used to write, compile, test, and deploy the smart contracts without local setup overhead. |
| Network | LACNet Open Pro-Testnet & EVM | Provides the EVM-compatible, zero-GAS-fee execution environment across the three regulatory jurisdictions (Peru, Ecuador, Colombia). |
| Gasless Transaction Layer | BaseRelayRecipient | Enables gasless meta-transactions so coastal cooperatives can interact with the protocol without holding native gas tokens. |
| MRV & Data Integrity | MRV Oracle Simulation & IPFS | Simulates drone-based hectare verification and pins the resulting imagery/data to IPFS for tamper-evident, decentralized storage. |
| Web3 Integration | Ethers.js | Bridges the frontend interface with the deployed smart contract, handling wallet connections, transaction signing, and event listening. |
| Frontend | HTML5 / CSS / JavaScript | Delivers the LACNet Protocol Dashboard: MRV panel, revenue distribution view, and live terminal log. |

### Smart Contract Architecture

The contract's state is organized around two core structures:

- **Balance mapping:** an `address => uint256` mapping associates each participant's wallet address with their accumulated TTF (tCO2eq) balance, updated exclusively through permissioned issuance functions.
- **Role-based access control:** a separate `address => bool` (or role-enum) mapping designates authorized "verifier" accounts — MRV specialists or automated oracles — who alone can trigger TTF issuance for a validated restoration event. This prevents self-reporting fraud and ensures every issuance event is both attributable and auditable on-chain.

Circular-economy functions burn or lock TTF tokens against a defined action catalog (corporate offset, nursery reinvestment, ecotourism/fishing-quota access), with each transaction emitting an event log that forms the immutable audit trail referenced in Section 2.

Releasing the full codebase, documentation, and smart contract source as open-source deliverables is not a peripheral choice; it is a deliberate signaling mechanism. ## Section 6: Impact & Scalability Justification

### Impact

By directly channeling protocol revenue to coastal cooperatives, Blue-Carbon TTF targets the value-chain exclusion identified in Section 2. In the pilot phase, the protocol has verified 40.0 hectares of restored mangrove, minted 860 TTF (tCO2eq) credits, engaged 9 active coastal cooperatives, and recorded a **22% increase** in black-shell (concha negra) capture associated with restored zones.



Every minting event is distributed automatically by smart contract as follows: Coastal Communities 55%, Technology/Platform 20%, MRV Verification 15%, and Nursery Reinvestment 10%.

### Scalability

The protocol was designed as multijurisdictional from inception: the MRV Oracle panel already supports parameterized regulatory frameworks for Peru (MINAM), Ecuador (MAE), and Colombia (MADS), allowing the same EVM-compatible contract to be deployed or forked across additional mangrove corridors without requiring a shared centralized registry. This allows a natural expansion path: from the Tumbes pilot, to a nationwide network of protected mangrove areas, and eventually to interoperability with **international voluntary carbon market registries** and **commercial blue carbon buyers**.

## Section 7: Alignment with Hackathon Goals

Blue-Carbon TTF was designed explicitly around the hackathon's core theme, **"Innovating for a Sustainable Future,"** by making the verified restoration of a threatened coastal ecosystem the sole source of economic value in the system.

**Alignment with UN SDGs:**

- **SDG 13 (Climate Action):** the protocol directly finances the conservation of a verified blue carbon sink, keeping millions of tonnes of stored CO2 out of the atmosphere.
- **SDG 14 (Life Below Water):** by channeling 55% of protocol revenue to coastal cooperatives, the system creates a direct economic incentive to protect the mangrove habitat that sustains crab, shrimp, and mollusk fisheries.

Choosing an open, decentralized public good — rather than a proprietary, closed registry — is the most ethical architecture available for blue carbon finance. Climate and biodiversity data of institutional or public interest should not be gatekept behind a private company's database; open verifiability is itself a public good, and it directly serves the accountability goals embedded in both SDGs above.
